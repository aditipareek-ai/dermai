About this condition

Atopic dermatitis is a chronic, relapsing inflammatory skin disorder defined by intense itch, dry skin, and recurrent eczematous lesions. Part of the atopic triad alongside asthma and allergic rhinitis. The condition stems from a disrupted skin barrier combined with a dysregulated immune response. While there is no cure, evidence-based treatments significantly reduce flare frequency and severity.

Recommended treatments
01
Apply fragrance-free emollient moisturizer immediately after bathing — minimum twice daily while skin is still damp
02
1% hydrocortisone OTC cream for mild flares — apply thinly once or twice daily on affected areas only
03
Use only gentle, pH-balanced, unscented soaps to minimize further barrier damage
04
Identify and avoid personal triggers — sweat, wool, dust mites, pet dander are common culprits
05
Consult a dermatologist for prescription calcineurin inhibitors or dupilumab if OTC options are insufficient
When to seek care
Signs of infection — yellow crusting, fever, rapidly spreading redness
Condition severely disrupting sleep or daily life
No improvement after 2 weeks of consistent OTC treatment
Condition affects face, eyelids, or other sensitive areas
Uncertainty about diagnosis — similar conditions need ruling out
